Energy Access in East Africa: 2017–2022

Data period: 2017–2022

Executive Summary

Between 2017 and 2022, all five focus countries in East Africa achieved steady gains in electricity access. Kenya led with a rise from 55.8% to 76.0% of its population connected, while Rwanda posted the second highest increase (34.1% to 50.6%). Ethiopia, Uganda, and Tanzania each recorded improvements of 11–15 percentage points, driven by a mix of grid extensions and off-grid solutions funded primarily by World Bank programs and regional development banks.

Insights

Ethiopia’s network reinforcement under ENREP boosted rural access by over 11 percentage points, with further private-sector incentives supporting expansion. Kenya’s significant 20-point gain reflects a dual approach of grid extensions and ambitious off-grid solar programs supported by government subsidies. Rwanda combined grid rollout with targeted off-grid solutions to increase access by 16.5 points, aligning with its 2034 universal access target. Tanzania’s TREEP initiative connected more than 4.5 million rural users, while Uganda’s Electricity Access Scale-up Project reached 16 districts.

Anomalies & Trends

  • Kenya stands out both for highest overall access and largest five-year increase.
  • Grid extensions dominate in Ethiopia and Uganda, while Rwanda and Kenya balance grid with off-grid solutions.
  • Tanzania’s progress under TREEP remains moderate, highlighting infrastructure financing needs.
  • Private-sector incentives and international credit lines are key drivers in Rwanda and Ethiopia.

Recommendations

  1. Scale up off-grid solar and mini-grids in underserved regions, replicating Kenya’s model.
  2. Enhance private-sector participation through incentives and streamlined regulatory frameworks.
  3. Expand follow-on phases of ENREP and TREEP to close the remaining rural access gap.
  4. Prioritize medium- and low-voltage infrastructure upgrades for reliability and resiliency.
  5. Foster regional knowledge sharing on successful financing mechanisms and subsidy schemes.
